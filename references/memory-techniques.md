# Memory Techniques for Speed Cramming

Evidence-based memorization methods optimized for short-time exam prep. These are NOT study methods — they are memorization hacks for when you need to stuff information into your brain fast.

---

## 1. Spaced Repetition Micro-Cycles (间隔重复微循环)

Traditional spaced repetition takes weeks. For cramming, use compressed micro-cycles.

### The 1-5-30-120 Rule

After learning something, review it at these intervals:
- **1 minute** — immediate recall check
- **5 minutes** — short-term consolidation
- **30 minutes** — medium-term check
- **2 hours** — same-day lock-in
- **Before sleep** — sleep consolidates memory

### Daily Micro-Cycle Schedule

```
Morning:   Learn new material (2 hours)
+1 min:    Quick self-test on what you just learned
+30 min:   Review while taking a break
Afternoon: Practice questions on the material
Evening:   Final review of all today's material
Before bed: Read cheat sheet once, then sleep
```

**Why it works:** Sleep is when the brain consolidates short-term memories into longer-term storage. Reviewing right before sleep maximizes this effect. Research shows a 40% improvement in recall with pre-sleep review vs. daytime-only review.

### The "Never Skip Night 1" Rule

The first night of sleep after learning is disproportionately important. If you sacrifice sleep to study more, you lose more from forgotten material than you gain from the extra study time.

## 2. Mnemonic Chains (助记词链)

For lists of items, create a story chain where each item triggers the next.

### How to Build a Chain

1. Take your list of items (e.g., 5 causes of X)
2. Create a vivid, absurd story connecting them in order
3. The more ridiculous, the better — the brain remembers unusual things

**Example:**
- Need to remember: TCP/IP layers → Application, Transport, Internet, Network Access
- Chain: "An **App**le was **Transport**ed across the **Internet** to a **Network** of computers"
- Mental image: a giant apple being trucked across the internet to a pile of computers

### When to Use

- Any ordered list (process steps, historical events, system layers)
- Lists of 5-15 items
- Items that must be recalled in sequence

### Tips

- Use your own language and cultural references — they're more memorable
- Involve multiple senses: sound, smell, touch in the story
- Practice the chain 3 times: write it, say it, visualize it

## 3. The Keyword-Image Method (关键词图像法)

For concepts that have specific terminology, attach each keyword to a vivid mental image.

### How It Works

1. Extract the key term (e.g., "mitochondria")
2. Find a similar-sounding word in your language (e.g., "密头" → 密码头 → a secret dock)
3. Create an image: a secret dock where energy (ATP) is being loaded onto ships
4. The image encodes both the term AND its function

### Chinese Exam Example

- 关键词: "诉讼时效" (limitation period)
- 谐音: "树杏十效" → 树上长着十个杏，每个杏代表一年
- 图像: 一棵树上挂着10个杏，树下有人数杏，过了10个杏就过期了
- 效果: 记住"诉讼时效=一般10年（民法）"

### When to Use

- Legal/medical/technical terminology
- Foreign language vocabulary
- Any terms that are hard to remember but frequently tested

## 4. Acronym / First-Letter Method (缩略词法)

Classic but effective. Turn lists into memorable acronyms or sentences.

### Rules for Effective Acronyms

1. **Make it meaningful** — the acronym itself should relate to the topic
2. **Make it absurd** — "My Very Educated Mother" is more memorable than "M V E M"
3. **Chinese-specific**: Use 首字组词 (first-character combination) or 谐音缩略

### Examples

- OSI 七层模型: "应表会传网数物" → "硬表会传网束物" (谐音: 硬币会传染网束物 → a coin can contaminate the net bundle)
- 中国历史朝代: "夏商周秦西东汉，三国两晋南北朝，隋唐五代又十国，辽宋夏金元明清"
- 会计六要素: "资负所权收费" → "自助所全收费" (谐音: 自助餐厅全部收费)

### When to Use

- Lists of 4-10 items
- Items where order doesn't matter
- Quick on-the-spot recall needed

## 5. Memory Palace / Loci Method (记忆宫殿)

For large amounts of structured information, map items to physical locations you know well.

### Quick Setup (10 minutes)

1. Choose a familiar route (your home → front door → living room → kitchen → bedroom → bathroom)
2. Assign 1 topic/concept per location
3. Create a vivid, absurd image of the concept IN that location
4. Walk through the route mentally 3 times

### Example

- Front door: 考点1 — 合同法基本原则 (a giant contract nailed to your front door)
- Living room: 考点2 — 合同成立要件 (two people shaking hands on your sofa while your TV plays a courtroom drama)
- Kitchen: 考点3 — 违约责任 (your kitchen on fire because someone broke their cooking promise)

### When to Use

- 10+ concepts that need to be memorized
- Exam with broad topic coverage
- You have at least 1 day before the exam

### Cramming Adaptation

Don't build a full palace — use your body instead:
- Head → Topic 1
- Shoulders → Topic 2
- Hands → Topic 3
- Stomach → Topic 4
- Knees → Topic 5
- Feet → Topic 6

This takes 5 minutes to set up and is surprisingly effective for 6-8 items.

## 6. Active Recall Cards (主动回忆卡)

Reading notes is passive. Forcing yourself to recall is active — and 2-3x more effective.

### How to Make Cram Cards

1. **Front**: Question / key term / prompt
2. **Back**: Answer / definition / formula

**Critical rules:**
- Write the QUESTION, not just the keyword ("What are the 3 elements of X?" not "Elements of X")
- Keep answers to 1-3 sentences max
- Include a "hook" — a mnemonic, image, or association

### Digital Alternative

If you don't have physical cards, use this format in a text file:

```
Q: [question]
A: [answer]
💡 Hook: [mnemonic/image/association]
---
```

### Review Protocol

1. Read the question
2. **Close your eyes and try to recall the answer BEFORE looking**
3. If correct → mark ✓, review again in 30 min
4. If wrong → mark ✗, review again in 5 min
5. At end of session, review all ✗ cards once more

## 7. The Feynman Flash (费曼闪念法)

For understanding (not just memorizing) a concept quickly.

### Steps (5 minutes per concept)

1. State the concept in plain language (no jargon)
2. If you can't explain it simply, you don't really know it — flag for cheat sheet
3. Find the ONE analogy that makes it click
4. Write the analogy on your cheat sheet, not the textbook definition

**Example:**
- Concept: "Opportunity cost"
- Simple: "What you give up when you choose something"
- Analogy: "Choosing to study means giving up sleep. The sleep is the opportunity cost."

### When to Use

- Concepts that keep appearing in practice questions but you keep getting wrong
- Understanding bottleneck topics that affect multiple questions
- NOT for rote memorization items (dates, names, formulas)

## Quick Selection Guide

| Your Problem | Best Technique | Time to Set Up |
|-------------|---------------|----------------|
| "I need to memorize a list" | Acronym / Mnemonic Chain | 5-10 min |
| "I keep forgetting terminology" | Keyword-Image Method | 10-15 min |
| "I have 20+ concepts to learn" | Memory Palace (body version) | 15-20 min |
| "I read it but can't recall" | Active Recall Cards | 20-30 min |
| "I sort of know it but keep getting it wrong" | Feynman Flash | 5 min per concept |
| "I need to remember everything I study today" | Spaced Repetition Micro-Cycles | 0 min (built into schedule) |
| "I have a formula sheet to memorize" | Mnemonic Chain + Active Recall | 15-20 min |

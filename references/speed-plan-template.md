# Speed Plan Templates

Pre-built study plans by available days. Select the matching template and customize for the specific exam.

---

## 3-Day Blitz (极限冲刺)

Use when: exam is in 1-3 days, zero to minimal preparation.

### Day 1: Reconnaissance (侦察日) — 4-6 hours
**Goal:** Know what you're up against.

| Time Block | Task | Duration |
|-----------|------|----------|
| Block 1 | Search exam format, passing score, question types | 30 min |
| Block 2 | Find 1-2 past papers or sample questions online | 1 hour |
| Block 3 | Skim past papers — identify the 5 most common topic areas | 1 hour |
| Block 4 | For each topic area: read ONE summary/guide (not the textbook) | 2 hours |
| Block 5 | Create a "cheat sheet" — key formulas, definitions, rules on 1 page | 30 min |

### Day 2: Tactics Day (战术日) — 6-8 hours
**Goal:** Practice exam-taking, not learning.

| Time Block | Task | Duration |
|-----------|------|----------|
| Block 1 | Learn meta-skills: elimination, keyword recognition, time management | 1 hour |
| Block 2 | Timed practice set 1 (half the exam length) | 1 hour |
| Block 3 | Review errors — identify pattern (knowledge gap vs. technique gap) | 30 min |
| Block 4 | Timed practice set 2 (full exam length) | 2 hours |
| Block 5 | Review errors — focus only on high-frequency mistakes | 30 min |
| Block 6 | Drill: rapid-fire 20 questions focusing on weak areas | 1 hour |
| Block 7 | Review cheat sheet, update with new patterns found | 30 min |

### Day 3: Final Push (终极日) — 3-4 hours max
**Goal:** Consolidate, don't expand.

| Time Block | Task | Duration |
|-----------|------|----------|
| Morning | Review cheat sheet 3 times | 30 min |
| Morning | Do 1 more timed practice set | 1 hour |
| Afternoon | Review errors from all practice sets | 30 min |
| Afternoon | Mental rehearsal: walk through exam strategy (3-pass method) | 15 min |
| Pre-exam | Light review of cheat sheet only — no new material | 15 min |

**3-Day Rules:**
- NEVER try to learn the entire subject — only the top 5 topics
- ALWAYS practice under timed conditions
- Prioritize technique over knowledge
- Sleep is non-negotiable — 7 hours minimum each night

## 7-Day Sprint (一周速成)

Use when: exam is in 4-7 days, some basic familiarity with the subject.

### Day 1-2: Intelligence Gathering (情报收集)
**Goal:** Map the battlefield.

- Day 1: Research exam format, find 3+ past papers, identify topic distribution
- Day 2: Skim all past papers, mark recurring topics, create topic priority list

### Day 3-4: Core Strike (核心突击)
**Goal:** Cover the top 3 highest-frequency topics.

- Day 3: Study topic #1 (most frequent) — use summary materials, NOT textbooks
- Day 4: Study topic #2 and #3 — same approach
- For each topic: read summary → do 10 practice questions → note errors

### Day 5: Tactics Training (战术训练)
**Goal:** Learn and practice exam techniques.

- Morning: Learn all meta-skills (elimination, keyword recognition, time management)
- Afternoon: 2 full timed practice exams
- Evening: Review all errors, categorize as knowledge-gap vs technique-gap

### Day 6: Gap Filling (查漏补缺)
**Goal:** Address the gaps found in practice.

- Morning: Fix knowledge gaps (only the ones that appeared in practice)
- Afternoon: Practice technique gaps (re-do questions you got wrong using correct technique)
- Evening: Create final cheat sheet

### Day 7: Final Review (终极复习)
**Goal:** Consolidate and prepare mentally.

- Morning: Review cheat sheet + error notes
- Afternoon: 1 final timed practice exam
- Evening: Light review only, early sleep

## 14-Day Standard (两周速成)

Use when: exam is in 8-14 days, want a solid pass.

### Phase 1: Recon (Day 1-3)
- Day 1: Research exam thoroughly (format, scoring, past papers, study resources)
- Day 2: Find and organize study materials (summaries, cheat sheets, NOT full textbooks)
- Day 3: Create study plan — map each day to specific topics

### Phase 2: Content Blitz (Day 4-9)
- Day 4-5: Topic cluster 1 (highest frequency topics)
- Day 6-7: Topic cluster 2 (medium frequency topics)
- Day 8-9: Topic cluster 3 (remaining topics)
- Daily rhythm: 2 hours study → 30 min practice questions → 30 min error review

### Phase 3: Tactics + Practice (Day 10-12)
- Day 10: Learn all meta-skills, practice with easy questions
- Day 11: 2 timed practice exams, full error analysis
- Day 12: Targeted drilling on weak areas

### Phase 4: Final Push (Day 13-14)
- Day 13: Final practice exam + error review + cheat sheet finalization
- Day 14: Light review + mental preparation + early sleep

## 30-Day Comfortable (一月冲刺)

Use when: exam is in 15-30 days, want higher confidence.

### Week 1: Intelligence + Foundation
- Research exam, collect materials, create comprehensive study plan
- Start with the most unfamiliar topics while energy is high
- Daily: 2-3 hours study + 30 min practice

### Week 2-3: Deep Coverage
- Systematic coverage of all major topics
- Daily: 3 hours study + 1 hour practice
- Weekly: 1 full timed practice exam
- End of week 2: mid-point review — adjust plan based on practice results

### Week 4: Tactics + Polish
- Learn and practice all meta-skills
- 3-4 full timed practice exams
- Focus on error patterns and weak areas
- Create comprehensive cheat sheet for final review

### Final 3 Days: Consolidation
- No new material — only review and practice
- Daily: 1 timed practice exam + error review
- Focus on confidence and mindset
- Early sleep, proper meals, light exercise

## Minimal Viable Plan (最小可行方案) 🆕

Use when: working full-time, only 1-2 hours per day available.

### Core Principle

**You can't cover everything. Accept it. Focus on maximum ROI activities.**

### Daily Time Budget: 1-2 Hours

| Time | Activity | Why |
|------|---------|-----|
| First 15 min | Review yesterday's cheat sheet / error notes | Spaced repetition lock-in |
| Next 45-60 min | Study ONE high-frequency topic | Depth on what matters most |
| Last 15-30 min | Do 5-10 practice questions on today's topic | Active recall > passive reading |

### Weekly Rhythm (7-Day Cycle)

| Day | Focus (1-2 hours) |
|-----|-------------------|
| Mon | Study Topic 1 + practice |
| Tue | Study Topic 2 + practice |
| Wed | Study Topic 3 + practice |
| Thu | Review Mon-Wed errors + practice |
| Fri | Study Topic 4 + practice |
| Sat | 1 timed practice exam (use 2-3 hours if you can) |
| Sun | Error analysis + cheat sheet update |

### Weekend Power Block

If you can carve out 3-4 hours on weekends:
- **Block 1 (90 min):** Full timed practice exam
- **Block 2 (30 min):** Error analysis
- **Block 3 (60 min):** Targeted study on weakest areas
- **Block 4 (30 min):** Cheat sheet update

### Special Rules for Working Professionals

1. **Commute time = audio review** — record yourself reading the cheat sheet, listen during commute
2. **Lunch break = 1 practice set** — even 15 minutes of practice questions counts
3. **Pre-sleep review is non-negotiable** — 10 minutes reviewing cheat sheet before bed
4. **Don't sacrifice sleep** — sleep deprivation at work + exam prep is a losing strategy
5. **Batch similar topics** — if you study related topics on consecutive days, retention is better
6. **Use your phone** — take photos of your cheat sheet, review during any downtime

## Custom Plan Generator

When none of the above templates fit exactly, use this formula:

### Step 1: Calculate available hours
- Total days × hours per day (realistic: 3-5 hours for full-time study, 1-2 hours for working people)
- Example: 10 days × 2 hours/day = 20 total hours

### Step 2: Allocate by phase

| Phase | % of Time | Activities |
|-------|----------|------------|
| Reconnaissance | 10% | Research exam format, find materials, create plan |
| Content | 40% | Study highest-frequency topics with summary materials |
| Tactics | 20% | Learn and practice exam techniques |
| Practice | 20% | Timed practice exams and error analysis |
| Final Review | 10% | Cheat sheet review, mental preparation |

### Step 3: Adjust for constraints

| Constraint | Adjustment |
|-----------|------------|
| Zero knowledge | +20% to Content, -10% from Tactics |
| High passing score (>80%) | +10% to Practice, -5% from Recon |
| Essay/subjective format | +10% to Tactics (template-based answers), -5% from Content |
| Practical/hands-on format | +15% to Practice, -10% from Content |
| <3 days available | Skip Recon, maximize Practice + Tactics |
| Working professional (≤2h/day) | Use Minimal Viable Plan template above |
| Mixed exam types | Allocate Tactics time by question-type proportion |

### Step 4: Generate daily schedule

For each day, output:
```
### Day X: [Theme] — [Total Time]
- [ ] ★ [Must-do task] — [Duration]
- [ ] [Optional task] — [Duration]
- 📝 Review: [What to review at end of day]
```

**★ = Must-do. If you only have time for one thing, do this.**
